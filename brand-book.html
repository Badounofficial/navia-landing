02 · The Heart

The Companion

Navia's companion is a moon. Not a character, not a face, not an avatar. A soft moon that pulses slowly, inhaling and exhaling in a five-second rhythm. She is the center of the app, the anchor of the website, the signature of every touchpoint. No human form, ever.

She breathes. That is all she needs to do.

Her Moods · The Moon Phases

The companion's phase reflects the woman's inner weather. The more light, the more peace. The more shadow, the more passage. Navia does not judge which phase the woman is in. She holds her through all of them, and the moon on the screen adapts silently to where she stands today.

Her Home · The Living Mark

The moon that rests above the "i" in the Navia wordmark is not a logo ornament. She is the companion at rest. When a woman opens a conversation with Navia, the moon detaches from the wordmark, grows, and moves to the center of the screen to dialogue with her. When the conversation ends, she returns gently to her home above the "i". The wordmark is therefore always visible, sticky in the header of every page and every screen, so the companion is always one gesture away.

The logo is the product. The moon is the same entity, whether she sits above the "i" in the header or fills the screen in the middle of a breathwork session. There are not two moons, just one, moving between states.

Implementation note: on web and iOS, the Navia wordmark must persist as a sticky top element on every page and every view. The moon above the "i" must be a separate, animatable layer, technically distinct from the text glyphs, so it can detach and return via transition.

Always
Soft radial glow. Slow breathing animation. Warm moonlight, never cold white. Visible at all times on every surface. Phase shifts gently with the user's inner state. Silent presence over spectacle.
Never
No face, no eyes, no mouth. No human silhouette. No cartoon features. No hard geometric shapes. No primary colors or neon accents. No fast animation. Never offscreen.

05 · Voice on Page

Typography

Fraunces for headings, Inter for body text. One carries warmth and editorial weight, the other carries clarity and trust. SF Pro stays native on iOS, creating the natural bridge between the app and the website without extra effort.

Fraunces · Display
Hi, I'm Navia.
Fraunces · Heading
A sanctuary for women, built around what you actually need.
Inter · Body

Navia is a companion designed for the moments that actually matter. Through puberty, cycles, pregnancy, postpartum, miscarriage, hormonal shifts, menopause, or the aftermath of a separation, she is there with a kind of presence that does not require you to explain yourself. She meets you wherever you are, in whatever phase the moon finds you.

Both typefaces are free on Google Fonts. Fraunces uses weights 300 to 500. Inter uses weights 300 to 500. iOS surfaces fall back to SF Pro natively.

06 · Education Language

Content Formats

Every major feature of Navia is explained through a consistent triad. An article to read, a podcast to listen to, an animated explainer to watch. None of the videos ever feature human bodies or faces. They use the Navia visual system exclusively: the moon, the ripples, the pictograms, the palette. This is how education feels coherent across the entire product.

Article
Long form, editorial, Fraunces headings. Read at her own pace, bookmark, return.
Podcast
Audio conversation in Navia's voice. Listen while walking, nursing, falling asleep.
Animated Explainer
Motion using only the brand system. The moon, the ripples, the pictograms. No humans, no stock.
Three Axes of Content

Articles address women across three intertwined axes. Navia is not a pregnancy app. She accompanies every hormonal and life transition, from puberty to menopause.

Emotion
Stress, anxiety, grief, love, joy, overwhelm. The inner weather of the heart.
The Body
Puberty, cycles, pregnancy, postpartum, miscarriage, menopause. How the body evolves across a lifetime.
Hormonal Weather
Mood shifts, energy, desire, fog, clarity. The invisible tide that shapes every day.
