# Ozaia — Brand In The World: 6 Ultra-Realistic Prompts

*How the Ozaia brand looks when it exists in the real world. Billboards, subway, gift cards, hotel lobbies.*

*All prompts optimized for Midjourney v6.1 `--style raw --s 50` or Flux Pro 1.1.*

---

## Brand Elements (consistent across all prompts)

- **Wordmark**: "Ozaia" in warm gold (#E8C58F) serif typography (Fraunces-inspired)
- **Moon-dot**: The dot on the "i" is a detailed full moon in warm ivory-gold with visible craters
- **Background**: Deep indigo (#2B2940)
- **Accent**: Soft rose (#E8A89D) for secondary text elements
- **Principle**: The Ozaia surface is always the quietest, darkest, most intentional element in any environment

---

## Prompt 1 — Times Square at Night

```
Photograph taken from street level in Times Square, New York City, at 11pm on a rainy night. Among the dozens of bright LED billboards, one massive screen on the side of a building displays the Ozaia brand: a deep indigo (#2B2940) background filling the entire screen, with a single word "Ozaia" in warm gold (#E8C58F) elegant serif typography centered on the screen, the dot on the letter i replaced by a detailed full moon rendered in warm ivory-gold with visible craters. Below the wordmark, a single line in small soft rose (#E8A89D) text, barely readable from this distance. No other graphics, no models, no product shots, just the wordmark and moon on indigo. The billboard stands out dramatically among the surrounding neon chaos because it is the only dark, quiet surface in the entire frame. The wet pavement below reflects the indigo glow and the gold wordmark as a shimmering mirror image on the ground. Yellow taxi cabs pass in the foreground, their headlights creating streaks of warm light. Pedestrians with umbrellas are silhouetted against the billboard glow, small in the frame. The contrast between the screaming colors of every other billboard and the deliberate silence of the Ozaia screen creates immediate visual tension. Shot on Canon EOS R5, 24mm f/1.4 wide angle lens, ISO 3200, 1/30s shutter to capture slight motion blur on the taxis and pedestrians. The rain adds thousands of tiny reflections and a cinematic atmosphere. Color temperature mixed: 6500K from the LED screens, 3200K from the street lights, the Ozaia billboard reads as the calmest point of light in the frame. Film grain from high ISO adds documentary realism. The image should look like it was shot by a street photographer who happened to notice this one quiet billboard and stopped to frame it. No watermark, no text overlay --ar 16:9 --style raw --s 50 --v 6.1
```

## Prompt 2 — NYC Subway Platform Ad

```
Photograph inside a New York City subway station, the 14th Street-Union Square platform on the L line. A large backlit advertising panel on the tiled wall displays the Ozaia brand: deep matte indigo (#2B2940) background with the word "Ozaia" in warm gold (#E8C58F) serif typography, the i dot replaced by a realistic full moon with visible craters and mare formations in warm ivory-gold tones. Below the wordmark, very small text in soft rose reads nothing more than a single short phrase, barely legible. The ad panel is flanked by the classic white subway tiles with dark green trim typical of Union Square station. The fluorescent station lighting creates a harsh blue-white cast on everything except the backlit Ozaia panel, which glows with warm indigo and gold, making it feel like a window into another world. A woman in a dark coat stands on the platform in front of the ad, seen from behind, her silhouette partially overlapping the panel, waiting for the train. She is slightly off-center, not posed. The yellow safety line on the platform edge is visible. A discarded coffee cup and a crumpled newspaper on the bench add reality. The tunnel to the left is dark, with two faint headlights of an approaching train visible in the distance. The tiles have natural wear, some slightly chipped. The ad is the warmest, most inviting element in an otherwise cold, utilitarian space. Shot on Leica Q3, 28mm f/1.7, ISO 6400, slight noise grain adding to the documentary feel. Available light only, no flash. The image should feel like a candid photograph from a photojournalism essay about New York at night. No watermark --ar 16:9 --style raw --s 50 --v 6.1
```

## Prompt 3 — Luxury Gift Card on Marble

```
Overhead flat lay photograph of an Ozaia gift card on a white Carrara marble surface with subtle gray veining. The card is thick heavy cotton stock, 3.5 x 5 inches, with rounded corners and a soft matte finish. The front of the card is deep indigo (#2B2940) with the word "Ozaia" letterpressed in warm gold foil (#E8C58F), slightly embossed so the letters catch the light with a subtle dimensional shine. The dot on the i is replaced by a small detailed full moon in gold foil, with micro-engraved crater details visible under the directional lighting. Below the wordmark, a thin line in soft rose gold foil. The card sits at a slight angle, casting a soft shadow on the marble. Beside the card, a small indigo envelope with a gold foil crescent moon seal closing the flap. A single dried rose petal in dusty pink rests near the bottom left corner. A thin gold ribbon, loosely curled, trails from under the card. The lighting is soft directional from the upper left, creating gentle shadows that reveal the letterpressing depth and the foil texture. The marble surface has natural imperfections and warm undertones. The overall feeling is quiet luxury, the kind of gift card you would receive from a brand that treats even its packaging as an intimate gesture. Shot on Hasselblad X2D 100C, 90mm f/3.2 macro lens, ISO 200, tethered to capture every texture detail. Color temperature 5400K, neutral with slight warmth. No text visible on the card other than "Ozaia". No watermark --ar 4:5 --style raw --s 50 --v 6.1
```

## Prompt 6 — PATH Train Car Interior Ad

```
Photograph inside a PATH train car between Newark and Manhattan, late evening, the car half-empty. An overhead interior advertising panel displays the Ozaia brand: deep indigo (#2B2940) background, the word "Ozaia" in warm gold (#E8C58F) serif typography with the full moon replacing the i dot, and a single short phrase in soft rose below. The ad is one of several panels running along the ceiling of the car, but it is the only dark one, all others being bright white with colorful graphics for dental clinics, language schools, and personal injury lawyers. The contrast makes the Ozaia panel look like a portal into a different atmosphere. A tired young woman in scrubs sits directly below the Ozaia ad, her head leaned back against the window, eyes closed, earbuds in, the overhead fluorescent light catching the edge of her cheekbone. She does not know the ad is above her but the visual alignment creates a powerful accidental composition: the quiet exhaustion of a real woman underneath a brand that was made for exactly this moment. The train car interior has the characteristic orange and brown seats of PATH, scuffed metal poles, the digital route map glowing near the doors. Through the windows, the tunnel lights streak as horizontal lines. Other passengers are sparse: a man reading a phone, a backpack on an empty seat. The fluorescent lighting is harsh and unflattering everywhere except where the backlit Ozaia panel casts its warm indigo-gold glow on the ceiling around it. Shot on Ricoh GR IIIx, 40mm f/2.8 equivalent, ISO 6400, program mode as if shot quickly from the hip. High ISO noise grain adds to the raw documentary feel. Available light only. The image should feel like it was taken by a commuter who noticed the composition and grabbed a quick shot. No watermark --ar 16:9 --style raw --s 75 --v 6.1
```

## The Brand Principle

In every image, Ozaia is the quietest surface in the frame. While everything else screams, Ozaia whispers. The brand does not compete for attention. It creates a pocket of stillness that draws the eye precisely because it refuses to shout.
