## Why this image works for Ozaia

- **The double moon** (real + screen) captures the core idea: Ozaia bridges the real and the intimate digital
- **No face performance** — she is not posing, not smiling at camera, not "using an app"
- **The collarbone gesture** — self-soothing, unconscious, the body language of someone who feels safe
- **Triangle of light** — moon → face → phone, the eye travels naturally through the story
- **No text needed** — the image communicates the concept without a single word
- **Universal but specific** — any woman can see herself in this moment
- **Editorial quality** — belongs in Vogue Living, not in an app store screenshot carousel
