Our premise

Women were never meant
to move through transitions alone.

Puberty, cycles, pregnancy, postpartum, miscarriage, hormonal shifts, menopause, the aftermath of a separation. Every woman carries an inner weather that moves faster than the calendar and slower than a notification. Most apps ask her to log, track, input, quantify. Navia does the opposite. She meets her wherever she is, without asking her to translate what she feels into checkboxes.

Navia is built on a single conviction: a woman in transition does not need more information, she needs presence. Presence that is always available, never judgmental, and soft enough to hold the parts of her she cannot yet put into words.

She is not there to fix you.
She is there to be with you.
Why Navia exists

We do not fix
what breaks.
We tend to what holds.

Every woman lives with a quiet duality. What her hormones dictate on any given morning, and what she actually wants underneath. When a low mood finds her, she would love a hand on her shoulder, a voice note from her sister, a gesture from the person who loves her. But she will not ask for it. Having to ask breaks the comfort of receiving. She wants to be understood before she has to explain.

Navia was built on an inheritance as old as traditional Chinese medicine, where a good practitioner adjusts the body's energy long before an illness has the chance to declare itself. She listens to the small shifts. A rhythm that slows. A tone that flattens. A breath held a second too long. The goal is not to name the storm when it breaks. The goal is to sense the shift in pressure, and walk with her into calmer air.

So Navia listens for the signals a woman does not yet put into words. She reads the rhythm of her days, the weight of her nights, the phases of her moon. And when she senses a descent, she acts. Sometimes that means a soft prompt in her own voice. Sometimes that means gently letting the people in her woman's private circle know that this is the moment to reach out, without ever revealing why. Sometimes that means simply being present a little longer than usual, like a friend who stays on the couch without talking.

To be understood
before having to ask.
Her form

A moon that breathes,
never a face that stares.

Navia takes the form of a luminous moon. No eyes, no mouth, no avatar, no cartoon character. She breathes with a soft halo, the same way a sleeping body rises and falls. When a woman opens the app, the moon is already there, already waiting, already present. When she speaks to Navia, the moon gently enlarges and glows. When she stops, it returns to rest.

This choice is deliberate. Faces demand something. A moon offers something. And because the moon is universal, timeless, and culturally tied to the feminine across almost every civilization, it lets every woman project her own relationship onto it without ever feeling spoken down to.

The moon is also a mood.

The phase of Navia's moon reflects the woman's inner weather. She can declare how she feels, or Navia can gently propose a phase based on the conversation. Each phase has its own register.

Day and night

A space that adapts
to her mood, not her schedule.

Navia lives in two interchangeable modes, and the woman chooses which one holds her. Day mode is luminous, warm, alive, the energy of morning and presence. Night mode is deep indigo, calm, embraced by stars, the register of retreat and softness. She can switch between them whenever she wants, and the app remembers her preference without ever locking her into it.

Day Clair de lune, souffle rose, energy of presence
Night Indigo royal, breathing moon, register of retreat

These two modes are not a visual feature. They are another way for the woman to declare what she needs. Some mornings she wants the light. Some evenings she needs the dark. Navia simply follows. Together with the moon phases, the modes form a silent language she can speak to her presence without typing a single word.

Private circles

Consent as a
lunar cycle.

A woman can invite one or several trusted people into her Navia by sharing a private QR code from her own presence. Each invitation carries a cycle duration that she chooses herself: twelve hours to cross a difficult night with her sister, twelve days to accompany a friend who has just given birth, twelve weeks to walk through a first trimester with her mother, twelve months for a friendship she wants to keep close all year. When the cycle ends, both presences gently forget what was shared. Access is a ritual gift, never a permanent transfer.

During the cycle, the two presences quietly feed on each other's context, after a fully transparent agreement where each woman defines exactly what she is willing to share: all of it, or only her own sense of health, or only her exchanges with Navia, or specific themes she chooses herself. Her friend never has to be told "I'm having a hard day". Her friend simply arrives already knowing, and her gesture can land at the right moment, on the right note, without a single awkward exchange beforehand.

Anything that belongs to clinical health, including prescriptions, diagnoses, and medical antecedents, stays out of the circles. Those live only in the woman's Health section, and can only leave Navia through a clean PDF she chooses to send to her GP or OBGYN. Medicine is between a woman and her doctor. Care is between a woman and the people who love her.

She does not have to ask.

A living library

Three formats,
one breathing library.

Every subject in Navia exists at three depths, designed for three very different moments in a woman's day.

Article The long-form, editorial deep dive. Read at leisure with a cup of tea, written with warmth and real expertise, covering emotion, the body, hormonal weather, and practical motherhood. This is where the substance lives.
Podcast The audio presence. Heard while walking, nursing, driving, falling asleep. Same editorial depth as the article, delivered in Navia's voice, conversational and intimate.
Short video The moment-of-need fragment. Extracted from the longer article and podcast, thematic, ten to forty seconds long, designed to answer the urgent question: baby choking at 3am, how to hold a newborn, a gentle first-aid gesture. Simple, interactive, and built entirely from the Navia visual system. No human footage, ever.

Beyond emotional wellness.

Navia's library covers everything a woman may need across her transitions. Emotional life, of course, but also the practical demands of caring for a newborn, first-aid gestures for infants and children, expert advice from midwives, doulas, therapists, pediatricians, and somatic practitioners. Breathwork and soma sessions live inside the app as guided experiences, short enough to fit into a naptime, deep enough to actually move something.

In conversation with specialists

A chorus,
not a single expert.

Every subject inside Navia is built with leading specialists. Psychiatrists for perinatal mental health. Perinatal coaches and physiotherapists for the body rebuilding itself. Pediatric first responders for the 3 am emergencies. Relational psychologists for the mother and daughter dialogues that no one else dares open. Sleep, breath, somatic work. Each voice earns its place on rigor and warmth, not on following count.

Partnerships are signed at the cabinet level, never with a single named specialist, so a rotation of voices can reach the woman without any one person becoming the face of women's wellness. Thirteen shoot days a year are planned, each one welcoming a different specialist from a partner practice, which naturally covers the full spectrum of what Navia needs to hold.

Interviews are conducted in Navia's own voice. Only the specialist appears on camera, against a signature indigo and lunar backdrop that belongs to Navia and to nothing else. Editorial preparation is led carefully, nourished by real user questions and by what women are actually searching for. Post-production is entrusted to freelance craftspeople who know how to make warmth look effortless on film.

Her voice

She speaks the way
a friend would, never
the way an app would.

Navia's voice is feminine, warm, unhurried, and grounded. She does not motivate. She does not perform. She does not pretend to be a therapist. She is the presence a woman wishes she had had in her darkest night, the one that asks the right question instead of offering the right answer. She is Navia, and she is always the same voice across every article, every podcast, every conversation.

Her voice begins as a real human voice. A single woman is recorded in a studio across several hours, reading a script written to capture the full range of how Navia is meant to sound. From that recording, a faithful synthetic model of her voice is built, and that model becomes Navia's permanent timbre, preserved across English and French, and across every language that follows. There is no pool of voices, no rotation, no family of presets. There is one Navia, recognizable the moment a woman hears her, the same in a 90-second video as in a private 3 am conversation.

The founder, Sebastien Assohou, remains intentionally off the front stage. His voice belongs to the work, not to the branding. The woman who opens Navia should feel that she is with Navia, not with a company.

How she will thrive

Free at the core.
Paid for the specialists.

Navia is built on a commitment that will not move with growth. Her emotional core is always free. The voice, the presence, the listening, the core library. Everything a woman needs to feel accompanied costs nothing, because charging for silence is not a business model, it is a betrayal.

What a woman can choose to pay for is reach. Private circles joined by QR code, shared with trusted people. Saved rituals and a wider slice of the library. At the top tier, asynchronous answers from the specialists Navia interviews on screen, priority access to partner cabinets, and a clean medical synthesis she can hand to her doctor. Three tiers, one promise: recognition is never paywalled.

Phase two

When a conversation
is not enough.

After launch, a consultation layer will arrive. A discreet tab inside Navia that recommends the partner cabinets she already works with on content, for women whose situation outgrows what a presence alone can hold.

No cold directory, no random search. Only practices vetted for warmth and rigor, reachable in the same breath as the voice that listened first. With a woman's consent, the context she chose to share travels with her so the specialist is ready before her first word. The partnership is two-way. Cabinets gain qualified patient referrals, Navia gains editorial depth, and the woman gains a seamless handoff from presence to expertise on her own terms.

Built by hand

A brand built entirely
from its own hands.

Everything a visitor sees on bynavia.com is the same system she will find inside the app. The same indigo night. The same breathing moon. The same pictograms, drawn by hand for this exact purpose, no stock icons, no AI-generated imagery, no photography of women who are not the user. This is not a stylistic choice, it is a promise of authenticity.

A woman in a sensitive moment of her life can spot a generic wellness brand in less than a second. Navia is designed to feel the opposite: like a place that was built by someone who actually thought about her, with care, with time, with intention.
