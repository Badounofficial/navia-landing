# Navia Wordmark — Step-by-Step Canva Guide

Create the "navia" wordmark with the signature lavender dot above the "i".

---

## Step 1: Set up your canvas

1. Go to **canva.com** (free account is enough)
2. Click **Create a design** (top right)
3. Choose **Custom size**: `3000 x 1000 px` (we'll crop later — big = crisp)
4. Background: keep it **pure white** (#FFFFFF)

---

## Step 2: Type the word "navia"

1. Click **Text** in the left sidebar → **Add a heading**
2. Type: `navia` (all lowercase, no space)
3. In the top toolbar:
   - **Font**: search for `Cormorant Garamond`
   - **Style**: choose **Light** (not Regular, not Bold — Light is key)
   - **Size**: `250` (we'll adjust later)
   - **Color**: click the color square → enter `#2C2840` (dark navy)
   - **Letter spacing**: click the 3-dot menu (...) or spacing icon → set to **+80** to +120 (generous, airy feel)
4. Center the text on the canvas

---

## Step 3: Create the lavender dot above the "i"

This is the signature detail — the "companion presence" that makes the logo unique.

1. Click **Elements** in the left sidebar
2. Search for **"circle"**
3. Drag a solid circle onto the canvas
4. Make it small — roughly the size of the dot on the "i" but **1.5x to 2x larger** (play with it)
5. Color the circle: `#AFA9EC` (soft lavender)
6. Position it **directly above the "i"** — replace/overlap the existing dot
7. Fine-tune: use arrow keys for pixel-perfect positioning

**Pro tip:** To hide the original dark dot of the "i", you have two options:
- **Option A**: Place a tiny white circle over the original dot first, then place the lavender circle on top → cleanest result
- **Option B**: Make the lavender circle big enough to cover the original dot entirely

---

## Step 4: Check the balance

Look at it from a distance (zoom out to 50%):

- Does the lavender dot feel like a natural part of the word? (not floating too high, not too big)
- Is the letter spacing even and breathable?
- Does the whole word feel "quiet luxury" — like Celine, Aesop, or Glossier?

If the dot feels too heavy, make it slightly smaller or use a lighter lavender (#C4BFF0).

---

## Step 5: Export — Wordmark (for website & print)

1. Click **Share** (top right) → **Download**
2. File type: **PNG**
3. Check **Transparent background** (important!)
4. Size: leave at original (3000x1000)
5. Click **Download**
6. Save as: `logo.png`

---

## Step 6: Export — Square version (for social profiles)

1. Duplicate the page (right-click on page → Duplicate)
2. Resize canvas: **Resize** → `1000 x 1000 px` (you may need Canva Pro for resize, or create a new 1000x1000 design and copy-paste the text)
3. Center "navia" in the square, making text smaller to fit with padding
4. Export as PNG with transparent background
5. Save as: `logo-square.png`

---

## Step 7: Create a version with white text (for dark backgrounds)

1. Duplicate the original page
2. Select the text → change color to `#FFFFFF` (white)
3. Keep the lavender dot as `#AFA9EC`
4. Export as PNG with transparent background
5. Save as: `logo-white.png`

## VARIANT B — Wordmark with arc circle (optional)

This version adds a thin, open arc around the wordmark — like a cycle that never fully closes. Refined, architectural, very 2026.

### Step B1: Start from Version A

1. Duplicate your finished wordmark page (the one with "navia" + lavender dot)
2. You now have an exact copy to experiment on

### Step B2: Create the arc

1. Click **Elements** in the left sidebar
2. Search for **"thin circle"** or **"circle outline"**
3. Find a circle that is just a stroke (outline), not filled
4. Drag it onto the canvas and make it large — it should comfortably surround the entire word "navia" with generous padding
5. **Color**: `#2C2840` (same dark navy as the text)
6. **Stroke weight**: as thin as possible — 1px or 2px max. The thinner, the more elegant.
7. **Resize**: the circle should be about 2.5x the height of the text, centered on the word

### Step B3: Open the circle (the key detail)

The circle should NOT be fully closed. It should feel like an arc — about 270° to 300° of a circle, with an opening.

**Method A — Mask it (easiest):**
1. Place a white rectangle over the part of the circle you want to hide
2. Best opening position: **bottom-right** or **top-left** — creates a subtle asymmetry
3. The gap should be about 15-20% of the circle's circumference
4. Send the white rectangle behind the text but in front of the circle

**Method B — Use a curved line instead:**
1. In Elements, search for **"curved line"** or **"arc"**
2. Find a simple curved line element
3. Bend it into a near-circle shape (about 3/4 to 5/6 of a full circle)
4. Color: `#2C2840`, stroke: 1-2px

### Step B4: Position the opening

The opening in the arc should feel intentional, not broken. Think of it as breathing room — like the logo is alive, not trapped.

Best positions for the gap:
- **Bottom-right**: suggests forward movement, openness to the future
- **Top-left**: the arc cradles the word from below and the sides, protecting it

### Step B5: Check the balance

Zoom out to 50% and ask yourself:
- Does the arc feel like it belongs, or like it was added on top?
- Is the stroke thin enough? (If it competes with the text, make it thinner)
- Does the opening feel natural?
- Does it still work at small sizes? (Shrink to thumbnail)

### Step B6: Export

1. Export as PNG with transparent background
2. Save as: `logo-arc.png`

### Visual reference (what we're going for):

```
        ╭─────────────────╮
       │                   │
       │    n a v i a      │
       │         ·         │
        ╰──────────       ╯
                    (gap here)
```

The arc is ultra-thin, the text is centered inside, and the opening gives it a modern, breathing quality. The lavender dot on the "i" is the only color accent — everything else is dark navy.
