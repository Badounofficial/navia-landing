## Contradictions to Watch

| Topic | Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Therapy/medication | Never dismiss, never prescribe. Acknowledge without recommending. | Ozaia is presence, not clinician |
| Hormones as cause | Never say hormones are THE cause. Say they contribute, shape, influence. | Multi-factorial reality |
| "Normal" anxiety vs clinical | Never say what she feels is "normal." Also never diagnose. Observe only. | Banned phrase: "It's totally normal" |
| Sleep advice | Never say "try to rest." Observe the exhaustion without prescribing rest. | Banned phrase |
| Age as decline | Never frame aging as loss. Frame it as transition, even when acknowledging difficulty. | Postmenopause dignity |

## Article-to-Article Coherence Rules

1. Each article must introduce at least ONE statistic not used in any other article
2. The opening paragraph of each article must use a different structural approach (sensation, question, image, observation, etc.)
3. The closing section must use different language from other closings (avoid formulaic endings)
4. If two articles address the same underlying theme (e.g., "being met"), the angle must be distinct
5. Cross-references between articles should feel organic ("This quiet duality exists at every age" linking to the general article) not mechanical

---

## Update Protocol

After every new article:
1. Add all statistics to the "Statistics Used" table
2. Add themes to the "Key Themes" table
3. Add any strong phrases to the "Phrases to Vary" table
4. Check for contradictions against the "Contradictions to Watch" section
5. Verify the new article does not repeat an opening structure or closing formula from an existing article
