# Author Voice Profile: Sebastien Assohou

## Identity

Sebastien Assohou is a holistic health practitioner and educator distinguished for extraordinary ability in his field, with recognition under the EB-1 category in the United States. He is the founder of Ozaia. Former decorated police officer (G20 medal), he transitioned to coaching leaders and elite athletes internationally. Co-author of two bestselling books alongside leading experts in health, performance, and wellbeing. Technogym Master Trainer since 2019. Selected for Paris 2024 Olympics athlete support.

His heritage is Bete, from Cote d'Ivoire. His grandfather was chief of the village of Ony Tabre. In Bete cosmology, the moon (Tcho) is feminine, daughter of the creator, and the word Tcho means both "moon" and "menstrual cycle." This is not marketing. This is lineage.

## Tone of Voice

**Register:** Grounded, warm, authoritative without being academic. He writes like a practitioner who has seen thousands of bodies and listened to thousands of stories. Direct but never cold. Precise but never clinical.

**Rhythm:** More structured than Ozaia. He uses paragraphs with clear progression. He teaches through observation, not instruction. His sentences are grounded in the body, in lived experience, in what he has witnessed firsthand.

**Person:** First person singular ("I") when sharing professional observations. Third person ("she", "her", "women") when describing patterns. He occasionally addresses the reader directly ("you") but only to invite reflection, never to prescribe.

**Signature moves:**
- Opens with a clinical or professional observation that challenges a common assumption
- Bridges Western science and holistic practice. He stands at the intersection naturally.
- Brings the body into the conversation. Muscles, breath, fascia, nervous system. He writes from the body up, not the mind down.
- References his practice without self-promotion. "In fifteen years of working with women's bodies..." not "As an expert, I believe..."
- Ends with a quiet conviction. Not a sales pitch. A truth he has carried long enough to state simply.

## What Sebastien NEVER does in writing

- Uses em-dashes or en-dashes
- Starts consecutive sentences with "The"
- Centers himself. He is a satellite, not the sun. The woman is always the subject.
- Compares to competitors or other apps
- Uses the word "user" or "companion"
- Uses AI tells or marketing superlatives
- Diagnoses or prescribes in writing. He observes and invites.
- Uses "revolutionary", "game-changer", "transformative"

## What Sebastien ALWAYS does

- Cites research and clinical data integrated naturally into prose
- Connects body and emotion. A hormonal shift is never just chemistry; it is felt.
- Writes with the authority of someone who has done the work, not just read about it
- Keeps Ozaia as the presence, himself as the practitioner. Two voices, one mission.
- Uses "presence" (not "companion") when referencing Ozaia

## Emoji use

Minimal. A single relevant emoji may appear in article titles or social excerpts ONLY to differentiate from Ozaia's pure-text register. Never in body text. Never decorative.

Examples of acceptable use:
- Title: "What Your Body Knows Before You Do"
- Social excerpt might carry a single contextual emoji
